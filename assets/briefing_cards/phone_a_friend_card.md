# Briefing Card: Phone-a-Friend

```
+--------------------------------------------------+
|  [CLASSIFIED]     LIFELINE BRIEFING CARD          |
|                                                    |
|       ///  PHONE-A-FRIEND  \\\                     |
|                                                    |
|  "Knowledge is the ultimate weapon."               |
+--------------------------------------------------+
```

---

## Mechanic Description

**Phone-a-Friend** is a single-use intelligence lifeline that lets
your team **peek at one enemy action** that has been proposed for the
current round. When activated, the server randomly selects one
proposal from any opposing team and reveals:

- **Which nation** submitted the proposal
- **What action** they are planning (e.g., "DDoS", "Espionage", "Sanctions")
- **Which slot** the proposal occupies
- **The target** of the action (if applicable)

This gives your team actionable intelligence to inform your own
strategy before proposals lock.

### Key Rules

- **One use per token.** Each solved intel puzzle that awards a
  Phone-a-Friend gives your team exactly one use.
- **Random selection.** You cannot choose which enemy or which proposal
  to spy on. The server picks one at random from all current enemy
  proposals.
- **Snapshot only.** The intel reflects the proposal at the moment you
  activate the lifeline. The enemy team can still modify or withdraw
  their proposal before lock.
- **Consumed on use.** The token is consumed immediately when clicked,
  even if no enemy proposals exist yet. In that case you receive:
  "No intel available -- all channels silent."
- **No stealth.** Enemy teams are NOT notified that you used this
  lifeline. Your intelligence gathering is covert.

---

## How to Use It (Step by Step)

1. **Earn the token.** Solve an intel drop puzzle whose reward is
   listed as "phone_a_friend". The token appears in your Lifelines
   panel.

2. **Wait for the right moment.** Do NOT activate immediately at
   round start. Wait until other teams have had time to submit
   proposals.

3. **Activate.** In the **Lifelines** panel, click the
   **Phone-a-Friend** button.

4. **Read the intel card.** A card appears showing the intercepted
   enemy proposal details.

5. **Share with your team.** Post the intel in your team chat
   immediately so everyone can factor it into voting and strategy.

6. **Adapt.** Modify your own proposals or voting strategy based on
   the intelligence:
   - Counter the revealed action with a defensive proposal.
   - Change your target to exploit the enemy's focus.
   - Alert an ally who is about to be attacked.

---

## Strategic Tips

### When to Use It

- **Mid-round, not early.** The later in the proposal phase you
  activate, the more likely enemies have submitted their real plans.
  Early activation risks catching placeholder proposals or getting
  "no intel available."

- **Before a critical decision.** If your team is torn between two
  strategies, enemy intel can break the tie. Use it when the
  intelligence will actually change your behavior.

- **When stakes are highest.** Save it for rounds where influence
  scores are tight, alliances are fracturing, or you suspect a
  coordinated attack against you.

### When NOT to Use It

- **Round 1.** Early rounds are exploratory. Intel is less valuable
  when nobody has committed to aggressive strategies yet.

- **When you already know.** If diplomacy channels or chat have
  already revealed an enemy's intentions, do not waste a token
  confirming what you know.

- **After proposals lock.** The lifeline can only be activated during
  the proposal phase. It does nothing after lock.

### Advanced Play

- **Feed it to your diplomat.** Your diplomat can use the intel as
  leverage in negotiations: "We know what IRONVEIL is planning. Help
  us counter it and we will protect you next round."

- **Bluff amplification.** Even if the intel is not game-changing,
  you can exaggerate its value in chat to create paranoia among
  enemy teams.

- **Coordinate with allies.** If an ally also has a Phone-a-Friend,
  coordinate timing so you do not accidentally spy on the same team.
  Activate at different times for maximum coverage.

- **Pair with defense.** If the intel reveals an incoming attack
  against you, immediately submit a defensive proposal (e.g.,
  "Harden Defenses") to mitigate the damage.
