# Briefing Card: False Flag Operation

```
+--------------------------------------------------+
|  [CLASSIFIED]     LIFELINE BRIEFING CARD          |
|                                                    |
|       ///  FALSE FLAG OPERATION  \\\               |
|                                                    |
|  "Make your enemies fight each other."             |
+--------------------------------------------------+
```

---

## Mechanic Description

A **False Flag** is a single-use lifeline that allows your team to
**disguise the attribution** of one of your cyber operations. When
activated on a proposal, the round resolution engine will report your
action in the world news feed as if it were performed by a different
nation of your choosing.

The target nation still suffers the full mechanical effects of your
action (influence loss, defense degradation, etc.), but the **blame**
is publicly attributed to the nation you selected as the scapegoat.

### Key Rules

- **One use per token.** Each solved intel puzzle that awards a False
  Flag gives your team exactly one use.
- **Must be attached before lock.** You can only apply a False Flag to
  a proposal while it is still in **draft** status. Once proposals lock
  for round resolution, no changes can be made.
- **Irreversible.** Once a False Flag is attached to a proposal, it
  cannot be removed or transferred to a different proposal.
- **Conditional activation.** The False Flag only fires if the proposal
  it is attached to wins your team's internal vote and proceeds to
  resolution. If the proposal is not selected, the token is still
  consumed.
- **Does not affect mechanics.** Only the news attribution changes.
  The actual attacker (your team) and the actual target are tracked
  internally for scoring and leaderboard purposes.

---

## How to Use It (Step by Step)

1. **Earn the token.** Solve an intel drop puzzle whose reward is
   listed as "false_flag". The token appears in your Lifelines panel.

2. **Draft a proposal.** Submit a cyber action proposal as normal
   (e.g., "Launch DDoS against CORALHAVEN").

3. **Attach the False Flag.** While the proposal is still in draft
   status, open it and look for the "False Flag" section. Select the
   nation you want to blame from the dropdown list.

4. **Confirm.** Click the **Attach False Flag** button. The proposal
   card will show a flag icon indicating the disguise is active.

5. **Vote and resolve.** Proceed with team voting as normal. If your
   proposal wins and resolves, the news feed will read something like:
   > "SHADOWMERE launched a devastating DDoS attack against CORALHAVEN"
   ...even though your team (e.g., IRONVEIL) actually did it.

---

## Strategic Tips

### When to Use It

- **Sow discord.** Blame a neutral nation to turn two of your enemies
  against each other. If CORALHAVEN thinks SHADOWMERE attacked them,
  they may retaliate -- weakening both while you stay clean.

- **Protect alliances.** If you need to strike an ally's neighbor but
  do not want to jeopardize your diplomatic relationship, blame a third
  party.

- **Deny escalation.** If you are already under diplomatic pressure or
  UN scrutiny, a false flag keeps your aggression off the public record.

### When NOT to Use It

- **Against a team with Phone-a-Friend.** If an enemy used their
  intelligence lifeline and already knows what you are planning, the
  false flag attribution will look suspicious and may backfire
  diplomatically.

- **On low-impact actions.** Do not waste a false flag on minor
  operations. Save it for high-profile strikes where attribution
  matters.

- **When you want credit.** Sometimes demonstrating strength openly
  is more valuable than hiding. A visible attack can deter future
  aggression.

### Advanced Play

- Coordinate with your diplomat: have them publicly accuse the
  scapegoat nation in chat *before* the round resolves to make the
  false flag more believable.
- Stack with a real attack from the scapegoat nation against the same
  target for plausible deniability.
- Remember: the UN Peace Council can still veto the underlying
  proposal regardless of attribution.
